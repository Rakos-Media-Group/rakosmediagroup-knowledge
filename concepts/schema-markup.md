# Schema Markup

## Definition

Schema markup is code added to a website to help search engines, AI systems, answer engines, and other machines understand the meaning of a page more clearly.

Schema markup uses a shared vocabulary to describe entities, page types, business details, services, products, articles, FAQs, reviews, offers, locations, people, organizations, and other important information.

In simple terms, schema markup tells machines what something is.

A page may visually explain a service to a person, but schema markup can help a machine understand that the page is a Service page, that the service is provided by a specific Organization, that the organization has a website, that the page includes FAQs, and that the content belongs within a larger business entity.

Schema markup is one way to implement structured data.

## Why It Matters

Search engines and AI systems do not understand websites the same way people do.

A person can read a page, look at the design, understand the layout, and infer the meaning of headings, buttons, testimonials, and service descriptions.

Machines need clearer signals.

Schema markup gives those systems a more direct way to understand what a page represents.

This matters because visibility now depends on more than keywords. Businesses need to be understandable across Google Search, Google AI Overviews, Bing, ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, maps, answer engines, social search, voice search, and future AI agents.

Schema markup can help machines understand:

* Who the business is
* What the page is about
* What service is being offered
* What organization published the page
* What questions are answered
* What products or offers exist
* What locations are relevant
* How the page connects to the larger website
* What public identity signals are connected to the brand

Schema markup does not guarantee rankings or AI mentions. It helps support machine understanding.

## Schema Markup and Structured Data

Structured data is the broader concept. Schema markup is one common vocabulary used to create structured data on websites.

Structured data means information is organized in a machine-readable format.

Schema markup gives that information a shared language.

For example, schema markup can describe:

* Organization
* LocalBusiness
* Person
* Service
* Product
* Offer
* Article
* FAQPage
* WebPage
* WebSite
* BreadcrumbList
* Review
* DefinedTerm
* CreativeWork

Each schema type gives machines a clearer description of what a page or entity represents.

A service page may use Service schema. A concept page may use DefinedTerm schema. A framework page may use CreativeWork schema. A page with questions and answers may use FAQPage schema as supporting markup.

Schema markup should match the visible content on the page.

## Schema Markup and Entity SEO

Schema markup supports Entity SEO by helping machines understand distinct entities.

Entity SEO focuses on clarifying what a business, person, service, location, product, concept, or brand is.

Schema markup can reinforce those entities with structured labels.

For example, schema markup may help identify:

* Rakos Media Group as the Organization
* Lisa Churakos and Chris Churakos as people associated with the business
* SEO as a Service
* Entity SEO as a DefinedTerm
* Search Everywhere Framework™ as a CreativeWork
* A knowledge page as a WebPage
* FAQs as FAQPage content
* Breadcrumbs as site structure

These signals help machines understand not only the page, but also the relationships behind the page.

## Schema Markup and Knowledge Graphs

Knowledge graphs depend on entities and relationships.

Schema markup can support a knowledge graph by making those relationships clearer.

For example, schema can help connect:

* An organization to its website
* A service to its provider
* A page to its publisher
* A concept to its definition
* A FAQ to the page it supports
* A framework to its creator
* A breadcrumb trail to website structure
* A public profile to the same business entity

This is why schema markup is important in Knowledge Graph Engineering.

It helps turn a website from a collection of pages into a more connected, machine-readable system.

Rakos Media Group’s Search Everywhere Graph™ uses this kind of thinking to organize businesses for modern search, AI search, answer engines, and future agents.

## Schema Markup and AI Search

AI search systems retrieve, summarize, compare, explain, and recommend information.

To do that well, they need clear context.

Schema markup may support AI search by helping systems understand the content and the entity behind it.

For example, when a page explains AI Search Optimization, schema markup can help clarify that the page was published by Rakos Media Group, that the page is part of a website, that the business offers related services, and that the content connects to related concepts.

AI systems do not rely only on schema. They also interpret visible content, links, citations, public mentions, reviews, and other signals.

Still, schema markup helps reduce ambiguity.

It gives machines a clearer starting point.

## Schema Markup and AEO

AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization.

Answer engines need clear information that can be retrieved and used to answer questions.

Schema markup can support AEO by making page context, FAQs, services, and organization information easier to understand.

FAQ schema can be useful when a page includes real question-and-answer content.

However, FAQ schema should not be used as a shortcut for weak content. The answers should be helpful, accurate, and visible on the page.

For Rakos Media Group, schema markup supports answer readiness by connecting definitions, service explanations, FAQs, and concept pages to the larger business entity.

## Schema Markup and GEO

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization.

Generative engines create summaries, comparisons, and recommendations based on retrievable information.

Schema markup can support GEO by helping generative systems understand page meaning and entity relationships.

For example, schema can help clarify:

* The publisher of the content
* The page type
* The service or concept being discussed
* The organization behind the service
* The relationship between FAQs and the page
* The structure of the website
* The relationship between services, concepts, and frameworks

Schema markup does not force generative engines to cite or recommend a business. It helps create a cleaner foundation for machine understanding.

## Schema Markup and Local Businesses

Schema markup is useful for local businesses because local search depends on clear identity, category, location, and service information.

A local business may use schema markup to clarify:

* Business name
* Website URL
* Service area
* Address when appropriate
* Phone number
* Business category
* Services offered
* Staff or founder information
* FAQ content
* Reviews when appropriate
* Breadcrumbs
* Local service pages

This can support Local SEO, Google Business Profile alignment, maps visibility, and AI-assisted local discovery.

Local schema should be consistent with the visible website content and public business information.

Conflicting signals can make the business harder for machines to understand.

## Common Schema Markup Mistakes

Schema markup should be accurate, useful, and aligned with the visible page.

Common mistakes include:

* Adding schema that does not match the visible content
* Using the wrong schema type
* Adding too much markup without a clear purpose
* Marking up claims that are not supported on the page
* Forgetting organization identity
* Forgetting breadcrumbs
* Using FAQ schema for weak or hidden content
* Creating inconsistent business information
* Treating schema as a replacement for content
* Forgetting to connect schema to the larger entity structure

Schema markup should clarify the page, not confuse it.

## FAQ

### What is schema markup?

Schema markup is code added to a website to help machines understand the meaning of a page, entity, service, product, article, FAQ, organization, or other type of information.

### Is schema markup the same as structured data?

Schema markup is a common way to create structured data. Structured data is the broader concept of organizing information in a machine-readable format.

### Does schema markup help SEO?

Yes. Schema markup can support SEO by helping search engines understand page content, entities, services, FAQs, products, local business details, and relationships more clearly.

### Does schema markup guarantee rich results?

No. Schema markup can make a page eligible for certain rich results, but it does not guarantee that Google or another system will display them.

### How does schema markup help AI search?

Schema markup helps AI systems understand who published the content, what the page is about, what entity or service is involved, and how the page connects to the broader website.

### What schema types are important for service businesses?

Common schema types for service businesses include Organization, LocalBusiness, Service, WebPage, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, Article, Person, and Review when appropriate.

### Why does Rakos Media Group use schema markup in knowledge graph work?

Rakos Media Group uses schema markup because it supports machine-readable business infrastructure, Entity SEO, AI Search Optimization, AEO, GEO, and Knowledge Graph Engineering.
