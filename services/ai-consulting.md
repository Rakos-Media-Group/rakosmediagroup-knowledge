# AI Consulting

## Definition

AI Consulting is the practice of helping a business understand, choose, implement, and use artificial intelligence in ways that support real business goals.

For Rakos Media Group, AI Consulting is not about chasing every new tool. It is about finding practical uses for AI inside the business, improving workflows, reducing repetitive work, strengthening decision-making, and building systems the team can actually use.

AI Consulting may include workflow review, AI use case planning, tool selection, team training, trained GPT development, AI agent planning, knowledge organization, content support, customer journey support, automation strategy, guardrail development, and AI visibility planning.

The goal is useful adoption, not software overload.

## Why It Matters

AI is changing how businesses search, sell, create, support customers, manage information, and make decisions.

But many businesses are stuck between two bad options.

One option is doing nothing because AI feels overwhelming.

The other option is adopting random tools without a clear use case.

Both create risk.

A business that ignores AI may fall behind competitors that learn how to move faster, answer customers better, organize knowledge, and improve marketing performance. A business that adopts AI without strategy may waste money, confuse the team, expose sensitive information, or build systems nobody uses.

AI Consulting matters because it helps the business slow down enough to make smart decisions.

## Core Areas of AI Consulting

### AI Readiness

AI readiness looks at whether the business has the information, workflows, tools, and team habits needed to use AI effectively.

Many businesses want AI before their knowledge is organized. If documents, service definitions, customer FAQs, internal processes, offers, and data are scattered, AI outputs may be inconsistent or unreliable.

AI readiness helps identify what needs to be clarified before deeper AI adoption.

### Use Case Planning

Use case planning identifies where AI can create meaningful value.

A good AI use case is specific. It has a clear business problem, a defined user, a repeatable workflow, an expected outcome, and a realistic level of risk.

Examples may include content support, customer service drafting, internal knowledge search, lead qualification support, reporting summaries, SOP assistance, sales enablement, product education, local SEO research, or website content planning.

### Tool Selection

AI tool selection helps the business avoid buying unnecessary software.

The best tool depends on the task, budget, privacy needs, team skill level, integration requirements, and expected output.

Sometimes the right answer is a simple trained GPT. Sometimes it is an AI agent. Sometimes it is better documentation, better prompts, better workflows, or better reporting before any new tool is added.

### Workflow Improvement

AI can help improve workflows when the workflow is clear.

Workflow improvement looks at repetitive tasks, handoffs, decision points, bottlenecks, and manual steps.

The goal is not to automate everything. The goal is to reduce unnecessary friction while keeping humans in control of important decisions.

### Trained GPT Development

A trained GPT can help a business create more consistent outputs for repeated tasks.

This may include brand-aligned content drafts, service explanations, proposal support, customer FAQ support, internal knowledge retrieval, sales enablement, marketing planning, or team training support.

A trained GPT works best when it has clear instructions, organized reference material, defined boundaries, and human review.

### AI Agent Planning

AI agents can support more complex workflows than a basic prompt or chatbot.

AI Agent planning should begin with the business problem, not the technology. The business needs to define what the agent should do, what tools or knowledge it needs, what human approval is required, and what the agent should not be allowed to do.

Rakos treats AI agents as workflow support systems, not magic employees.

### Knowledge Organization

AI becomes more useful when the business knowledge is organized.

This may include services, offers, FAQs, sales language, customer objections, policies, content libraries, internal documents, product details, industry information, and public entity information.

Knowledge organization supports AI Consulting, AI Agents, AI Search Optimization, and Knowledge Graph Engineering.

### Team Enablement

AI adoption depends on people.

A business may need training, examples, usage guidelines, review processes, and shared expectations. Team members need to know when to use AI, when not to use AI, how to check outputs, and how to protect sensitive information.

AI should increase team confidence, not create chaos.

### AI Guardrails

AI guardrails help reduce risk.

Guardrails may include rules around privacy, client data, approvals, publishing, factual review, brand voice, tool access, sensitive topics, and human oversight.

Guardrails are especially important for healthcare, finance-adjacent services, legal-adjacent services, ecommerce, customer communication, and internal operations.

### AI Visibility

AI Consulting can also support how a business appears in AI search systems.

This connects to AI Search Optimization, AEO, GEO, entity clarity, structured data, and knowledge graph work.

A business using AI internally should also understand how AI systems may interpret it externally.
