# SEO

## Definition

SEO, or Search Engine Optimization, is the practice of improving a website so search engines can understand it, rank it, and show it to the right people at the right time.

For Rakos Media Group, SEO is not only about rankings. It is about helping a business become findable, understandable, useful, and trusted across the full customer journey.

Modern SEO includes technical structure, content strategy, on-page optimization, internal linking, local visibility, authority signals, structured data, entity clarity, user experience, and conversion alignment.

SEO is one part of a larger visibility system. It supports AEO, GEO, AI Search Optimization, Knowledge Graph Engineering, paid media, website development, and conversion strategy.

## Why It Matters

People use search when they have a question, a problem, a need, a comparison to make, or a purchase decision in front of them.

A business that does not show up during those moments loses opportunities to competitors that are easier to find and easier to understand.

SEO matters because it helps a business appear when potential customers are actively looking for information, services, products, solutions, or local providers.

Good SEO does more than bring traffic. It brings qualified traffic.

The right SEO strategy helps answer the questions customers already have, organize services in a way people and machines can understand, and create a clearer path from discovery to action.

Search has also changed. Google is still critical, but customers now search across AI systems, maps, social platforms, directories, review sites, and answer engines. That means SEO cannot live in isolation anymore. It must connect to a broader Search Everywhere strategy.

## Core Areas of SEO

### Technical SEO

Technical SEO helps search engines crawl, render, index, and understand a website.

This includes site speed, mobile usability, indexability, redirects, canonical tags, crawl paths, sitemap structure, broken links, page experience, and clean site architecture.

Technical SEO does not replace good content, but weak technical structure can prevent good content from performing.

### On-Page SEO

On-page SEO improves the content and HTML elements of individual pages.

This includes page titles, meta descriptions, headings, body content, image alt text, internal links, URL structure, schema markup, and content clarity.

For Rakos, on-page SEO should always support the user’s intent. A page should not simply repeat keywords. It should answer the reason behind the search.

### Content Strategy

SEO content should help customers make better decisions.

Strong content explains the problem, defines the topic, answers common questions, compares options, addresses objections, and connects the reader to the next step.

Content should also support topical authority. A business should not publish random posts just to have activity. It should build connected content around the services, industries, locations, questions, and buying decisions that matter.

### Local SEO

Local SEO helps businesses appear for location-based searches.

This may include Google Business Profile optimization, local landing pages, reviews, service area clarity, local content, local schema, directory consistency, and location-based internal linking.

For local businesses, local SEO is often one of the most valuable parts of search visibility.

### Entity SEO

Entity SEO helps search engines understand the business as a real organization with relationships.

This includes the business name, services, founders, locations, industries, public profiles, credentials, sameAs links, reviews, content, and structured data.

Entity SEO is important because modern search systems do not only read keywords. They interpret meaning, relationships, and trust signals.

### Structured Data

Structured data helps machines understand what a page represents.

Schema markup can identify organizations, services, people, local businesses, products, reviews, FAQs, articles, offers, events, and defined terms.

Structured data should support the content on the page. It should not be used to make claims that are not visible or true.

### Internal Linking

Internal linking helps both users and search engines understand the relationship between pages.

A strong internal linking strategy connects service pages, industry pages, blog posts, framework pages, concept pages, and conversion pages.

Internal links help distribute authority and guide customers through the decision journey.

### AEO and GEO Support

SEO now supports AEO and GEO.

AEO, or Answer Engine Optimization, helps content answer direct questions clearly.

GEO, or Generative Engine Optimization, helps content become easier for AI systems to summarize, cite, and recommend.

SEO provides the foundation. AEO and GEO expand that foundation for answer engines and generative AI systems.

## FAQ

### What is SEO?

SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. It is the practice of improving a website so search engines can understand it, rank it, and show it to people searching for relevant information, products, services, or solutions.

### Is SEO still important with AI search?

Yes. SEO is still important because AI search systems rely on many of the same signals that make a website understandable and trustworthy. Strong SEO supports AEO, GEO, and AI Search Optimization.

### What is the difference between SEO and AI Search Optimization?

SEO focuses on improving visibility in search engines. AI Search Optimization expands the work to include answer engines, generative AI systems, entity clarity, structured data, retrieval readiness, and recommendation visibility.

### What is the difference between SEO and AEO?

SEO helps a website rank and earn organic visibility. AEO helps content answer direct questions in a way answer engines can use. AEO builds on SEO.

### What is the difference between SEO and GEO?

SEO improves search visibility. GEO focuses on how a business may appear in AI-generated answers. GEO depends on clear content, authority signals, structured information, and entity understanding.

### Does SEO only mean writing blog posts?

No. Blog content can support SEO, but SEO also includes technical optimization, service page structure, internal linking, local visibility, schema markup, content strategy, and conversion alignment.

### How does local SEO fit into SEO?

Local SEO helps businesses appear for searches connected to a place, service area, or nearby customer need. It includes Google Business Profile optimization, reviews, local landing pages, local schema, and consistent business information.

### Why does Rakos connect SEO to conversion strategy?

Traffic alone does not create growth. Rakos connects SEO to conversion strategy so search visitors can understand the offer, trust the business, and take the next step.
